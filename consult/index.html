Product Management

What is a product manager, anyway? My view: in a deep sense, he or she is responsible for the success of a product.

A bit more concretely, as a product manager, one must make sure to build:

  • the right product...
  • for your users / customers...
  • at the right time...

For a more detailed answer, I highly recommend Marty Cagan's 2005 essay "Behind Every Great Product: The Role of the Product Manager". It's spot-on, in my opinion.

My Services

Here's a (non-exhaustive) account of what I do as a product manager:

Strategy

  • Decision framework development (how do you decide what software to build?)
  • Competitive and market analysis, model-building.

Planning

  • Breaking projects into manageable, actionable pieces.
  • (In collaboration with engineering teams) estimating project timelines.

Specification

  • Detailing requirements with exacting clarity (I believe strongly in "user stories").

Project management

  • Managing iterative development and test processes.
  • Tracking progress towards milestones.

Measurement

  • Selecting and tracking meaningful success metrics.

Team building

  • Help to find and hire great product managers.
  • Coaching and mentoring staff product managers.
