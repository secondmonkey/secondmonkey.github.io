What is product management, anyway?

A product manager is - in a deep sense - responsible for the success of his or her product. A bit more concretely, I like to think of product managers as being charged with ensuring that their organization produces:

  • the right product...
  • for your users / customers...
  • at the right time...

For a more detailed answer, I highly recommend Marty Cagan's 2005 essay "Behind Every Great Product: The Role of the Product Manager".

My Services

Here's a (non-exhaustive) account of what I typically do as a product manager:

Strategy

  • Competitive analysis.
  • Decision framework development (how do you decide what software to build?)

Planning

  • Breaking projects into manageable chunks.
  • (In collaboration with engineering teams) forecasting resource needs and project durations.

Specification

  • Detailing requirements with exacting clarity (I believe strongly in "user stories").
  • Visually specifying interactions, interfaces, and processes.

Project management

  • Managing iterative development and test processes.
  • Tracking progress towards milestones.

Measurement

  • Selecting and tracking meaningful success metrics.

Team building

  • Help to find and hire great product managers.
  • Coaching and mentoring staff product managers.

I love doing this job. But I also love helping organizations develop their own expertise in product product management, so I approach all of this with an eye towards helping establish product management as a discipline, with repeatable processes, templates, examples, etc.
